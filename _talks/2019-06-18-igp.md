The ability of the newest ECMWF reanalysis, ERA5, to represent polar mesoscale cyclones (PMCs) is assessed for the first time by comparing it to its predecessor, ERA-Interim.
We apply an objective vorticity-based algorithm to identify PMCs over the North Atlantic over 18 extended winters.
The higher spatiotemporal resolution of ERA5 and better data assimilation allow for an improvement in capturing mesoscale cyclones, as indicated by a track-to-track comparison with an observational dataset and exemplified by an observed PMC case.
We demonstrate that our tracking algorithm shows limited sensitivity to time resolution, especially when used with ERA5.
ERA5 reproduces the spatial distribution of cyclones more closely to that of recent satellite-based climatologies, as it resolves vorticity field with higher fidelity.
A track density maximum near Svalbard and a cyclogenesis hot spot over the Barents Sea are clearer in ERA5, while in ERA-Interim they are shifted to the Norwegian and Greenland Seas.
