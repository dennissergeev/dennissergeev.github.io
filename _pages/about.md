My main scientific interest is planetary atmospheric dynamics.
Most of my current research focuses on the atmospheric modelling of terrestrial exoplanets.
I hope to contribute to our understanding of the climate regimes and potentially habitable conditions on different planets.

At present, I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the College of Engineering, Mathematics and Physical Sciences, University of Exeter, UK.
I am a member of the [Exeter Exoplanet Theory Group (EETG)](https://www.exoclimatology.com), where we use an hierarchy of numerical models to study various exoplanet atmospheres.

Prior to arriving in Exeter, I obtained a PhD in Meteorology under the supervision of Prof. Ian Renfrew at the University of East Anglia, UK.
My PhD research was focused on high-latitude mesoscale cyclones, known as polar lows, and how their occurrence and evolution are affected by orography and sea ice in the Arctic.
I completed my BSc-equivalent degree, Specialist Diploma in Meteorology, at the Department of Meteorology and Climatology, Moscow State University, Russia.
